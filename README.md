# APSC 403: Hands On Electric Machines

This repository contains supporting files, simulations, CAD models, videos, Arduino code, and documentation for the final project completed for APSC 403: Hands On Electric Machines during Spring 2026 at William & Mary.

The course focused on the design, simulation, fabrication, and testing of a three phase sensorless axial flux brushless DC (BLDC) motor using a rotor stator rotor (RSR) YASA style architecture. The project combined electromagnetic theory, ANSYS Maxwell simulations, iterative CAD design, 3D printing, winding fabrication, and experimental motor testing.

One of the primary goals of the project was to investigate the feasibility of producing a functional electric motor using accessible manufacturing methods and materials, including the use of ferrite magnets as an alternative to rare earth magnets.

# Project Overview

The project began with the study of permanent magnet electric machine theory, including magnetic fields, magnetic materials, torque production, and motor topologies. Experimental activities involving ferrite magnet fabrication and magnetic field visualization using the MMC5983MA magnetometer were also performed.

The course then transitioned into electromagnetic simulations using ANSYS Maxwell. Both 2D and 3D simulations were used to analyze magnetic field behavior, ferrite bobbins, winding configurations, and magnetic material models.

The second half of the project focused on the iterative design and fabrication of a fully 3D printed axial flux BLDC motor. Multiple revisions of the rotor disks, stator assembly, housing, and support structures were developed throughout the semester in order to improve manufacturability, tolerances, and motor performance.

The final prototype successfully demonstrated rotational motion and validated the overall axial flux BLDC motor concept developed during the course.
